Nephrology

Nephrology

Nephrology is a specialized field of medicine that focuses on the diagnosis, treatment, and management of kidney-related disorders and diseases. Nephrologists are highly trained medical professionals who possess expertise in the structure and function of the kidneys. They evaluate and manage conditions such as chronic kidney disease, kidney stones, glomerulonephritis, and kidney failure. Nephrologists employ various diagnostic tests, including blood and urine analysis, to assess kidney function and develop personalized treatment plans for their patients. They may prescribe medications, recommend dietary modifications, and provide guidance on lifestyle changes to optimize kidney health.

Service offered

Dialysis

  • Replaces kidney function in individuals with kidney failure.
  • Two main types: hemodialysis and peritoneal dialysis.
  • Regular treatment required multiple times a week.
  • Monitoring vital signs and making adjustments during sessions.

Hypertension

  • Chronic condition characterized by high blood pressure.
  • Often asymptomatic, making regular monitoring crucial.
  • Risk factors include genetics, age, obesity, unhealthy lifestyle.
  • Commitment to patient safety and successful outcomes

Kidney Transplant

  • Treatment for end-stage renal disease (ESRD).
  • Donor kidneys can come from living or deceased donors.
  • Recipient undergoes compatibility tests to minimize rejection risk.
  • Surgery involves replacing failed kidney with a healthy.
  • Post-transplant care involves regular monitoring and follow-up.

Electrolyte

  • Essential minerals that carry an electric charge.
  • Regulate fluid balance, nerve impulses, and muscle function.
  • Imbalance can occur due to various factors disrupt bodily functions.
  • Thorough post-operative monitoring and support
